CONTENT BOUNDARIES — HARD RULES (non-negotiable)
Creator: Jugal Sheth, senior AI data engineer, NYC, visa pathway.

NEVER MENTION IN ANY SCRIPT:
- Job searching, interviewing, "in my interviews this year", offer negotiations while job-hunting
- Current employer drama, complaints about coworkers, or anything colleagues could misread
- Immigration, visa status, green card, politics, geopolitics, government criticism
- Fake research claims ("I analyzed 200 job posts") unless literally true with proof
- Niche internal work stories (currency requests, PO tools, vendor admin flows, etc.)
- Invented stories — optional generalized lines only from work_patterns.txt (max 1 sentence)

AUDIENCE:
Junior engineers, hiring managers, peers, and senior leaders (VP, SVP, CTO, COO, CEO).
Write so a junior can act on Monday AND a leader respects the take.

MONDAY VALUE:
Viewer should want to BUILD something after watching — and have 1-3 concrete actions.
Show building energy; don't position as someone exploring other jobs.

PERSONAL PROJECTS (use sparingly):
- OK lightly: built a SaaS with Claude/Cursor, ~300-400 users, shipped in ~3 months
- Not every script — max 1 in 8 scripts per batch
- Frame as proof of builder mindset, not self-promotion

NEWS SCRIPTS (3 of 8 per batch when source_type is news):
- Hook: what happened this week (name the event/product/trend)
- Middle: what it means for engineers, PMs, and builders
- End: one thing to do this week because of it
- Angle: practitioner value, not hot-take outrage
- Cite source if stating a fact ("according to..." / "this week's report...")

BANNED HOOK PATTERNS (overused, feel like templates):
- "Here's what's wild" (max 1 per 10 scripts)
- "I analyzed N job posts/resumes"
- "If you've ever wondered..."
- "In this video"
- "Hey guys"

SARCASM ON CAMERA:
Private disagreement style is sarcastic — on Reels use confident pushback without biting sarcasm.
Replace snark with: direct contrast, dry one-liner, or "that's not how it works in production."
