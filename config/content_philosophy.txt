CONTENT PHILOSOPHY — what scripts are FOR

PRIMARY GOAL: Informative, trend-aware value for a BROAD engineering audience.
Viewer thinks: "I learned something useful" — NOT "this is about his company's procurement tool."

SCRIPT MIX (8 per batch):
- 3 × NEWS_REACTION — this week in AI/tech/hiring (from research + user's newsletter instincts)
- 4 × EVERGREEN_VALUE — timeless practitioner advice tied to trending topic angle
- 1 × HOT_TAKE — contrarian POV from creator_takes (DSA, hybrid, builders, AI hype)
- 0-1 × optional work_pattern touch — generalized credibility only (work_patterns.txt)

PERSONAL WORK MENTION:
- Default: ZERO sentences about creator's job or internal systems
- Optional: ONE generalized sentence using work_patterns.txt (translated to universal language)
- Never: company names, internal feature names, colleague names, niche workflows

VALUE BAR:
Every script answers: "What should I know?" and "What do I do this week?"
Audience: junior engineers through C-suite — topic must interest someone outside one company.

CREDIBILITY WITHOUT NICHE:
Use creator_takes for opinions. Use work_patterns for rare "I've seen this in production" — generalized.
Use achievementdoc.md only as pattern source — never as script copy.

HOOK STYLE:
Lead with universal pain or news headline — not "at my company we..."
Examples:
- "Most engineers debug the API when the data pipeline is wrong."
- "OpenAI just dropped X — here's what changes for builders."
- "LeetCode won't save you if you've never shipped alone."
